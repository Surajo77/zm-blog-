The Surajo Habibu Ibrahim blog focuses on the mineral wealth of Zamfara State in Nigeria and the controversies surrounding its management.

Zamfara's significant gold resources have led to economic and legal disputes, especially with oil-producing states. The establishment of a gold reserve in Zamfara and the Central Bank of Nigeria's purchase of this gold have been criticized as potentially unconstitutional.

This arrangement is seen as giving Zamfara undue control over its resources, in contrast to the shared oil revenues from the Niger Delta. These issues have fueled debates about resource control and the need to restructure Nigeria's mineral resource management laws, highlighting regional tensions over fair resource distribution and governance.
